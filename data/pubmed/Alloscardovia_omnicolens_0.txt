Alloscardovia omnicolens emerging presence in premature rupture of membranes.

JM(2), Gutiérrez-Fernández J(2)(3).

Author information:

Alloscardovia omnicolens is a recently-reported microorganism with unknown
pathogenic implications. It has been isolated in various clinical localizations
but not in the endocervix. We isolated A. omnicolens in an endocervical sample
from a 31-yr-old patient with preterm premature rupture of membranes (PPROM) in
week 33+3 of pregnancy. The main risk of PPROM is prematurity and the possibility
of developing infectious chorioamnionitis, which can be lethal for the mother and
newborn. This is the first report of an association between A. omnicolens and
PPROM, although its pathogenic role has not yet been elucidated.
