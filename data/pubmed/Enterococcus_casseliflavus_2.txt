Enterococcus casseliflavus is a commensal bacterium present in the intestinal
microbiota of different animals. Previous studies have found that strains
isolated from livestock are often resistant to many different antibiotics. Here, 
we present three E. casseliflavus strains, UFMG-H7, UFMG-H8, and UFMG-H9,
isolated from urine collected from healthy dairy heifers in Brazil.
