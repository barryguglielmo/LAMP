To increase the knowledge about S. capitis in the neonatal setting, we conducted 
a nationwide 3-month survey in 38 neonatal intensive care units (NICUs) covering 
56.6% of French NICU beds. We demonstrated 14.2% of S. capitis BSI (S.capBSI)
among nosocomial BSIs. S.capBSI incidence rate was 0.59 per 1000 patient-days. A 
total of 55.0% of the S.capBSIs were late onset catheter-related BSIs. The S.
capitis strains infected preterm babies (median gestational age 26 weeks, median 
birth weight 855 g). They were resistant to methicillin and aminoglycosides and
belonged to the NRCS-A clone. Evolution was favorable in all but one case,
following vancomycin treatment.
