Presence and Concentrations of Select Bacterial Vaginosis-Associated Bacteria Are
Associated With Increased Risk of Pelvic Inflammatory Disease.

Haggerty CL(1), Ness RB(2), Totten PA(3), Farooq F(1), Tang G(4), Ko D(5), Hou
X(5), Fiedler TL(5), Srinivasan S(5), Astete SG(3), Fredricks DN(5).

In a vaginal 16S ribosomal RNA gene quantitative PCR study of 17 pelvic
inflammatory disease (PID) cases and 17 controls who tested positive for
Chlamydia trachomatis, women who additionally tested positive for Atopobium
vaginae, Sneathia spp., Megasphaera spp., Eggerthella-like bacterium or
Prevotella amnii were more likely to develop PID.
