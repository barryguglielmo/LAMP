
1. Tetanus Toxoid.

Rabadi T(1), Brady MF(2).
In: StatPearls [Internet]. Treasure Island (FL): StatPearls Publishing; 2020-.
2020 May 6.

Author information: 
(1)St. James School of Medicine
(2)University of Tennessee

The tetanus toxoid is an FDA approved vaccination given alone or in conjunction
with other vaccines. It is protective against effects from a gram-positive
bacillus, Clostridium tetani.  This bacteria produces a neurotoxin
called tetanospasmin, which blocks the release of an inhibitory neurotransmitter 
and leads to unopposed muscle contractions and spasms.[1]
